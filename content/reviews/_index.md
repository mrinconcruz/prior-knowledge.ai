Avoid, skim, borrow, buy, relive, or gift. I believe in making reviews functional. I use the following guidelines both to clarify my own thinking and help others decide how to approach the vast range of books, games, and films out there.

- **[0] - avoid:** there are only costs, no benefits
- **[1] - skim:** look up the wikipedia summary, or a review somewhere
- **[2] - borrow:** check it out from the library and read it
- **[3] - buy:** you want to own, read, and annotate it
- **[4] - relive:** you want to own, read, annotate, and re-read this one
- **[5] - gift:** so good you’ll want to share it with others


Most cultural products are 0s or 1s. They are either not worth the time, or worth the minimal time to know what is *in* a book. I’d say in some cases it is worth *paying* someone to give you a detailed summary and avoid the work itself!

Some works are 2s—that is, you should borrow the book, film, or game because a summary won't do them justice. Good academic works are 3s—these are books that you read and annotate. Their ideas are worth referencing in the future. They are the building blocks of your library.

Some works are worth reliving—these are the rare 4s. Their core concepts and ideas are fantastic, and come wrapped in a great aesthetic. Each re-experience is more enriching. As Marie Kondo says, they “spark joy.” These are worth getting in special editions as part of a prized collection. Anything earning a 5 meets a threshold of such virtuosity that it is an intellectual and aesthetic gift for friends and family. There are different types of 5s, of course. I have gifted *The Story of Your Life and Others* over 15 times, while books like *Qianlong* or games like *Hollow Knight* only once or twice. Know your audience!

Given my limited time on this earth, I mostly review works I think will be 4s or 5s. The 0s, 1s, and 2s I review are usually highly recommended or popular works that did not live up to their promise.
